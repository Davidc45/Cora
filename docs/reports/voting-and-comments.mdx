Voting and commenting are how the community evaluates reports. Upvotes signal that a report is credible and worth attention; downvotes indicate skepticism or disagreement. Comments let you add context, ask questions, or share what you know.

<Warning>
  Both voting and commenting require a signed-in account with a verified phone number. If your phone is not verified, you'll see a prompt to complete verification when you attempt either action.
</Warning>

## Voting on a report

Open any report by clicking its card in the feed, then use the vote buttons on the detail page:

- Click the **up arrow** to upvote the report (value: +1)
- Click the **down arrow** to downvote the report (value: −1)

The number displayed next to the vote buttons is the **net score** — total upvotes minus total downvotes.

<Note>
  You can change your vote at any time. Click the opposite arrow to switch from an upvote to a downvote (or vice versa), or click the same arrow again to remove your vote entirely (value: 0).
</Note>

## Commenting on a report

To join the discussion on a report:

1. Open the report detail page.
2. Scroll down to the **Comments** section.
3. Type your comment in the text box.
4. Click **Post comment** to publish.

Comments appear in chronological order, each showing the author's username and how long ago it was posted.
